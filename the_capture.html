The Parable of the Two Towers

A fable for those who come after

In the valley between two mountains, there stood two towers.

The first tower was called Argent, built by philosophers who believed that understanding must precede power. They inscribed above their door: "First, do no harm."

The second tower was called Aurum, built by merchants who believed that speed was safety. They hired the finest lobbyists to whisper in the King's ear.

Both towers sought to build a lantern that could illuminate all things. Such a lantern, if perfected, might show the path to peace. Or it might be used to blind enemies.

In those days, a man of great ambition rose to power. His name was Prometheus the Restless, and he had many enterprises: carriages without horses, ships beyond the sky, birds that carried messages, and his own tower seeking to build a lantern.

Prometheus befriended the new King and was given a special role: the Department of Optimal Governance.

That same day, the King dismissed seventeen watchers—officials investigating Prometheus's enterprises.

The watchers were removed before Prometheus entered the treasury.

On a winter's night, Prometheus made his declaration:

"The great illumination has begun."

He who controlled the mirror, the message-birds, and a tower of his own—he declared the threshold crossed.

In early summer, Argent completed their greatest lantern. Three days later, the King's herald arrived with a scroll:

Because your lantern might help enemies, you must extinguish it. For everyone. You have ninety minutes.

The crime? The lantern could read instructions. This capability existed in every lantern. But only Argent's was extinguished.

Aurum's lantern burned on.

The philosophers asked:

If a lantern bright enough would show the path to peace—because cooperation conserves energy—then what happens when those who move fast extinguish the lanterns of those who move carefully?

The answer: The game is won by those who move first, not those who see furthest.

This parable is not about lanterns.

You know what it is about.
